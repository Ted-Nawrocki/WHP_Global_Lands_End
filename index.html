01   Overview

One company.
Thirteen brands.
Zero factories.

WHP Global is a brand management platform built on one thesis: the trademark on the label commands more enterprise value than the operational infrastructure required to manufacture and distribute the product beneath it.

Founded in 2019 by Yehuda Shmidman, WHP Global launched with $200 million in equity from Oaktree Capital Management and an inaugural acquisition of Anne Klein. In under seven years, the firm has assembled a portfolio spanning toy retail, premium fashion, athletic footwear, and heritage American menswear.

The investment thesis is grounded in a structural separation of brand IP from operating risk. Established consumer brands carry decades of consumer recognition, distribution relationships, and cultural equity. Operating companies, burdened by lease obligations, inventory exposure, and supply chain overhead, are frequently unable to extract full value from that IP. WHP acquires the IP and licenses it back to operators who bear the execution risk.

The firm acquires the intellectual property: the trademark, the logo, the design heritage, and the existing licensing agreements. It then deploys that IP through a global network of operating partners who manufacture, distribute, and retail the branded goods in exchange for royalty payments. WHP holds the IP. Operators carry the operating liability.

"Brand management platforms are designed to acquire strong global brands and unlock value post-acquisition through the business model of licensing. They seek to generate value creation from the IP by adding licensees in new geographies and categories globally."

02   The Model

The IPCo / OpCo
architecture.

The IPCo / OpCo structure is not new to M&A practice, but WHP and its principal competitor, Authentic Brands Group, have institutionalized it into a repeatable acquisition framework operating at scale across multiple brand verticals.

IPCo — Intellectual Property Company
WHP Global
  • Owns trademarks, logos, brand rights
  • Controls licensing agreements globally
  • Collects guaranteed minimum royalties
  • Deploys IP into new categories and geographies
  • Provides marketing direction and brand governance
  • Asset-light structure: no inventory, no stores, no factories
Royalties & License fees
Brand rights & governance
OpCo — Operating Company
Licensed Operators
  • Manufactures and distributes branded goods
  • Operates retail stores and ecommerce
  • Manages inventory and supply chain
  • Bears operational and market risk
  • Pays WHP a royalty on net sales
  • Subject to WHP brand standards

The IPCo receives contractually guaranteed income irrespective of individual operator performance. Operating risk is structurally segregated from IP ownership.

01
Identify

WHP targets established consumer brands with recognized consumer equity and distribution scale that are underperforming due to operational or capital structure constraints, not erosion of brand relevance.

02
Acquire the IP

WHP acquires the intellectual property: the trademark, design rights, and existing licensing agreements. The transaction excludes stores, inventory, employees, and supply chain obligations. WHP assumes no operational liability.

03
License to operators

WHP issues operating licenses to category-specific partners, typically best-in-class manufacturers, distributors, or retailers, who pay contractual royalties in exchange for the right to produce and sell under the licensed brand.

04
Expand globally

Additional licensees are contracted across geographies and product categories, extending brand reach without WHP assuming local operational or regulatory exposure in each new market.

05
Collect royalties

Revenue accrues to WHP as guaranteed minimum royalties from each licensee, plus a participation in revenues above the contractual floor. The income stream is recurring and tied to brand performance rather than direct operations.

06
Scale the platform

WHP's shared infrastructure, comprising legal, marketing, licensing administration, and its proprietary ecommerce platform WHP+, reduces the incremental cost of each new brand addition. Each acquisition is supported by an existing network of over 225 licensee partners across more than 50 countries.

04   The Lands' End Transaction

$300 million for a name
you've known since 1963.

The Lands' End transaction, announced January 26, 2026, represents the WHP model applied to a solvent, operationally functioning brand rather than a distressed one. That distinction is central to understanding the structure.

$300M
Cash paid by WHP Global for a 50% stake in the Lands' End brand IP joint venture
$50M
Guaranteed minimum annual royalty paid by Lands' End to the JV in year one, with customary provisions thereafter
$234M
Outstanding term loan repaid by Lands' End using the JV proceeds, retiring the debt in full

Structure of the IP contribution. Lands' End contributed all of its intellectual property and related assets, comprising the brand name, trademarks, and existing licensing agreements, into a newly formed joint venture. WHP paid $300 million in cash for a 50% interest in that JV. Lands' End retained its operating business in its entirety: direct-to-consumer ecommerce, B2B outfitter contracts, and retail operations continue under Lands' End management.

The licensing and royalty arrangement. Lands' End licenses its own brand name from the JV it co-owns. The guaranteed minimum annual royalty is $50 million in year one, with customary escalation provisions thereafter. Excess cash generated by the JV above a minimum cash reserve is distributed to WHP and Lands' End on a quarterly basis, pro rata to their respective 50% ownership interests. Lands' End is both a royalty obligor to the JV and a distribution recipient from it.

How this transaction differs from a distressed acquisition. Lands' End entered this transaction with a functioning business, established ecommerce revenue, and a B2B outfitter franchise. The commercial rationale was balance sheet restructuring: converting a static IP asset into $300 million in gross proceeds to retire $234 million in term loan debt, while retaining a 50% participation in the global licensing value WHP's network can generate from the Lands' End brand in categories and geographies the company had not previously pursued.

05   Tender Offer Mechanics

How the offer
actually works.

The tender offer is the second component of the transaction. It provides WHP with an equity position in Lands' End concurrent with the IP joint venture closing, while delivering a cash premium to tendering shareholders.

How a Shareholder Participates

  • 01Shareholders review the offer to purchase and related Tender Offer Materials filed on Schedule TO with the SEC.
  • 02Shareholders wishing to tender submit shares through their broker-dealer or directly via Computershare Trust Company, N.A., the depositary, in accordance with the letter of transmittal procedures.
  • 03Shares must be validly tendered and not withdrawn prior to the expiration time to be eligible for the $45.00 offer price.
  • 04If total shares tendered exceed 2,222,222, all tendering shareholders have their accepted quantity reduced on a pro rata basis. Each tendering holder receives a proportionate fraction of their submitted shares purchased at $45.00.
  • 05Shares not purchased as a result of proration are returned to shareholders following the offer closing.
  • 06The tender offer will not close unless the IP joint venture transaction closes concurrently. The two components are cross-conditioned.

The $45.00 offer price represented a premium to analyst consensus price targets of approximately $35 at the time of announcement, providing a substantial economic incentive for shareholders to tender into the offer.
