01   Overview

One company.
Thirteen brands.
Zero factories.

WHP Global is a brand management platform founded on a single insight: the name on the label is often worth more than everything required to put a product on a shelf.

Founded in 2019 by Yehuda Shmidman, WHP Global launched with $200 million in equity from Oaktree Capital Management and a first acquisition in Anne Klein. In under seven years, the firm has assembled a portfolio spanning toy retail giants, premium fashion houses, athletic brands, and heritage American menswear.

The core premise is straightforward. Established consumer brands carry decades of recognition, customer loyalty, and cultural equity. Operational companies — retailers, manufacturers, distributors — are frequently unable to monetize that value efficiently, burdened by store leases, inventory cycles, and overhead. WHP separates the two.

The firm acquires the intellectual property: the trademark, the logo, the design heritage, the licensing agreements. It then deploys that IP into a global network of operating partners who manufacture, distribute, and retail the branded goods in exchange for royalty payments. WHP owns the name. Operators own the problem of getting products to customers.

"Brand management platforms are designed to acquire strong global brands and unlock value post-acquisition through the business model of licensing. They seek to generate value creation from the IP by adding licensees in new geographies and categories globally."

02   The Model

The IPCo / OpCo
architecture.

The industry term for what WHP does is the IPCo / OpCo split. It is not a new concept, but WHP and firms like Authentic Brands Group have scaled it into a systematic acquisition strategy.

IPCo — Intellectual Property Company
WHP Global
  • Owns trademarks, logos, brand rights
  • Controls licensing agreements globally
  • Collects guaranteed minimum royalties
  • Deploys IP into new categories and geographies
  • Provides marketing direction and brand governance
  • Asset-light — no inventory, no stores, no factories
Royalties & License fees
Brand rights & governance
OpCo — Operating Company
Licensed Operators
  • Manufactures and distributes branded goods
  • Operates retail stores and ecommerce
  • Manages inventory and supply chain
  • Bears operational and market risk
  • Pays WHP a royalty on net sales
  • Subject to WHP brand standards

↑ The IPCo receives income regardless of whether individual operators succeed. Risk is structurally insulated.

01
Identify

WHP targets established consumer brands with strong heritage and loyal customer bases that are underperforming due to operational or financial burden — not because the brand itself has lost relevance.

02
Acquire the IP

WHP purchases the intellectual property — the trademark, design rights, and existing licensing agreements. It does not acquire stores, inventory, employees, or supply chain obligations.

03
License to operators

WHP grants operating licenses to category-specific partners — best-in-class manufacturers, distributors, and retailers — who pay royalties in exchange for the right to produce and sell under the brand name.

04
Expand globally

New licensees are added across geographies and product categories. A brand acquired for its US retail presence can be extended into Japan, India, or the Middle East without WHP taking on local operational risk.

05
Collect royalties

Revenue flows back to WHP as guaranteed minimum royalties from each licensee, plus a share of excess revenue above the floor. The result is a largely predictable, recurring income stream tied to brand performance, not operations.

06
Repeat at scale

WHP's shared platform — legal, marketing, licensing infrastructure, ecommerce via WHP+ — reduces the marginal cost of adding each new brand. Scale is the structural advantage; each acquisition leverages the same network of 150+ existing licensee partners.

04   The Lands' End Transaction

$300 million for a name
you've known since 1963.

The Lands' End deal, announced January 26, 2026, is a textbook execution of the WHP model applied to a solvent, functioning brand — not a distressed one. That distinction matters.

$300M
Cash paid by WHP Global for a 50% stake in the Lands' End brand IP joint venture
$50M
Guaranteed minimum annual royalty paid by Lands' End to the JV in year one, with customary provisions thereafter
$234M
Outstanding term loan repaid by Lands' End using the JV proceeds, retiring the debt in full

What Lands' End did. Lands' End contributed all of its intellectual property and related assets — the brand name, trademarks, and existing licensing agreements — into a newly formed joint venture. WHP paid $300 million in cash for a 50% stake in that JV. Lands' End retained its operational business entirely: direct-to-consumer ecommerce, B2B outfitter contracts, and retail operations continue under Lands' End management.

The royalty arrangement. Lands' End now licenses its own brand name from the JV it half-owns. The guaranteed minimum royalty starts at $50 million in year one. Any excess cash generated by the JV above a minimum cash balance is distributed to both WHP and Lands' End quarterly, pro rata to their 50/50 ownership. Lands' End is simultaneously a payer into the JV and a recipient of distributions from it.

Why this is a different deal from a bankruptcy acquisition. Lands' End is not distressed. It entered this transaction with a functioning business, real ecommerce revenue, and a durable B2B outfitter franchise. The motivation was balance sheet optimization — converting a static IP asset into $300 million in liquidity to retire $234 million in term loan debt — and participating in the global licensing upside WHP's network can generate from the Lands' End brand in categories and geographies Lands' End itself never pursued.

05   Tender Offer Mechanics

How the offer
actually works.

The tender offer is the secondary component of the transaction, providing WHP with a meaningful equity stake in Lands' End while returning cash to selling shareholders at a premium.

Offer Terms

Purchaser entity LEWHP, LLC (indirect subsidiary of WH Topco, L.P., d/b/a WHP Global)
Target company Lands' End, Inc. (NASDAQ: LE)
Offer price $45.00 per share, cash
Maximum shares 2,222,222 shares
Maximum aggregate $100,000,000
Proration Yes — if oversubscribed, shares purchased pro rata
Offer commenced February 26, 2026
Initial expiration March 26, 2026 (11:59 p.m. ET)
Post-close ownership ~7% of shares outstanding
Condition Conditioned on closing of IP joint venture
Filing Schedule TO filed with SEC, February 26, 2026

How a Shareholder Participates

  • 01Shareholders review the offer to purchase and related Tender Offer Materials filed on Schedule TO with the SEC.
  • 02Shareholders who wish to tender submit shares through their broker or directly via the depositary in accordance with procedures described in the letter of transmittal.
  • 03Shares must be validly tendered and not withdrawn prior to the expiration time to be eligible for the $45.00 offer price.
  • 04If total shares tendered exceed 2,222,222, all tendering shareholders have their accepted quantity reduced pro rata — meaning each tendering holder receives a proportionate fraction of their tendered shares purchased at $45.00.
  • 05Unpurchased shares (those subject to proration reduction) are returned to shareholders after the offer closes.
  • 06The offer does not close unless the IP joint venture transaction also closes. The two components are conditioned on each other.

The $45.00 offer price represents a premium above analyst consensus price targets of approximately $35 at the time of announcement — a meaningful incentive for shareholders to tender.
